Word-based Passcode
 
(Please enable JavaScript)
 
How long? Use 5 words for a passcode used on a closed network, 7 words for one used out on the Internet, 9 words for protecting an encrypted document or archive, or a couple words for a PIN, random email, or username.
 
To use: Type the first 3 letters of each word. If needed to meet the passcode security rules, uppercase the first letter and type the hyphen(s).

CVC Passcode
 
The CVC (consonant-vowel-consonant) pattern works well for a Wi-Fi password because it's easy to share with someone. It's also pronounceable, so it may be easier to remember. Use 5, 7, or 9 CVC's depending on how publicly visible the Wi-Fi network is.

Base-32 Passcode
 
Use Base-32 for passcodes required to be 20 characters or less. It's shorter, but harder to remember. Use 12 characters on a closed network, 16 on the internet, or 20 for an encrypted document or archive. It may be typed in lowercase; it's shown using both upper- and lowercase characters to make it less ambiguous to write down, especially if the zero and uppercase Z are written with slashes.
 
Base-32 uses characters from this set: [0123456789aBcdeF,HijkLmnopQR,t,vwx,z].
