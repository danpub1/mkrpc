Base-32 Passcode

(Please enable JavaScript)

Use Base-32 for a passcode required to be 20 characters or less. It's shorter, but harder to remember.

How long? Use 12 characters on a closed network, 16 on the internet, or 20 for an encrypted document or archive.

To use: It may be typed in lowercase and without hyphens; it's shown using both upper- and lowercase characters to make it less ambiguous to write down, especially if the zero and lowercase Z are written with slashes. Or type it in mixed case to meet passcode security rules if needed. The lowercase letters are all the vowels, plus the consonants in "doze mint joke".

Base-32 uses characters from this set: [0123456789aBCdeF,HiJkLmnopQR,t,VWX,z].
