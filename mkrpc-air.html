mkrpc: Make Random Passcode

Below are some random passcodes generated on your device, for use wherever you need a passcode. Refresh the page to get a new set.

Use a word-based passcode for most purposes. Use Base-32 for a passcode required to be 20 characters or less, since it's shorter for the same level of strength. Or mix and match: add a 4 character Base-32 "word" to a word-based passcode for 20 bits more strength.

Word-Based Passcode

(Please enable JavaScript)

How long? Use 5 words for a passcode used on a closed network, 7 words for one used out on the Internet, or 9 words for protecting an encrypted document or archive. Use 5 or more words for a Wi-Fi passcode, depending on how publicly visible the Wi-Fi network is. Use a couple words for a PIN, random email, or username.

To use: It may be typed in lowercase, and without spaces or hyphens. If needed to comply with passcode security rules, uppercase the first letter and type the hyphen(s).

How long? Use 12 characters on a closed network, 16 on the internet, or 20 for an encrypted document or archive.

To use: It may be typed in lowercase and without hyphens. It's only shown using both upper- and lowercase characters to make it less ambiguous to read and to write down, especially if the zero and lowercase Z are written with slashes. Or type it in mixed case to meet passcode security rules if needed.

Base-32 uses characters from this set: [0123456789aBCdeF,HijkLmnoPQR,t,VWX,z]. The lowercase letters spell "mint adze joke", a funny story about a pale green woodworking tool. There's no room for "guys", though. Those four letters are not used.
